Kolli Hills

  1. Located in the Eastern Ghats, Kolli Hills is a hidden gem known for its untouched natural beauty and spiritual significance.
  2. Offers stunning viewpoints like Agaya Gangai Waterfalls, Seekuparai Viewpoint, and Selur Nadu.
  3. Known for the Arapaleeswarar Temple, an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, attracting pilgrims and devotees.
  4. Provides opportunities for trekking enthusiasts with numerous trails, including the famous Agaya Gangai trek.
  5. Features medicinal plant farms, where visitors can learn about traditional herbal remedies.

Meghamalai

  1. Situated in the Western Ghats, Meghamalai, also known as the High Wavy Mountains, is a pristine hill station.
  2. Offers breathtaking views of mist-covered mountains, tea plantations, and cardamom estates.
  3. Known for its abundant wildlife, including elephants, gaur, and spotted deer, making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts.
  4. Features the Meghamalai Falls and Vellimalai, where visitors can enjoy scenic walks and capture memorable photographs.
  5. Provides a tranquil environment away from the crowds, ideal for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Topslip

  1. Located in the Anaimalai Hills, Topslip is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Indira Gandhi Wildlife Sanctuary.
  2. Offers thrilling wildlife safaris and elephant rides to explore the rich biodiversity of the region.
  3. Known for its teak forests, bamboo groves, and abundant bird species, making it a haven for birdwatchers.
  4. Provides opportunities for nature walks, trekking, and camping amidst the pristine wilderness.
  5. Features the Elephant Camp, where visitors can interact with and learn about these majestic creatures.
