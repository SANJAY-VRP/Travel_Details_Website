Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park:

  1. Marine Biodiversity: Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park is a marine biodiversity hotspot and one of the country's richest coastal ecosystems. It is home to vibrant coral reefs, seagrass meadows, and a wide array of marine species, including endangered sea turtles, dolphins, and dugongs.
  2. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: The park offers thrilling opportunities for snorkeling and scuba diving, allowing visitors to explore the underwater world teeming with colorful corals, tropical fish, and other fascinating marine life. It is a paradise for diving enthusiasts and nature photographers.
  3. Conservation Initiatives: Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park plays a vital role in conserving fragile marine ecosystems. Efforts are made to protect coral reefs, conduct research on marine species, and raise awareness about sustainable fishing practices and the importance of preserving marine biodiversity.
  4. Pamban Island: The park includes Pamban Island, which is famous for its iconic Pamban Bridge connecting Rameswaram to the mainland. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the sea, visit the revered Ramanathaswamy Temple, and experience the unique blend of natural and cultural attractions.
  5. Dolphin and Sea Turtle Watching: The park is known for its thriving populations of dolphins and sea turtles. Boat tours are available to spot these magnificent creatures in their natural habitats, providing an unforgettable wildlife encounter.
