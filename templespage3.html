Ekambareswarar Temple, Kanchipuram:

  1. The Ekambareswarar Temple in Kanchipuram is dedicated to Lord Shiva as Ekambareswarar. Here are five key points about this ancient and revered temple:
  2. Vast Temple Complex: The temple complex sprawls over 25 acres and is one of the largest temples in Kanchipuram. It consists of several halls, shrines, and sacred water tanks.
  3. Thousand Pillar Mandapam: The temple features the Thousand Pillar Mandapam, which is adorned with intricately carved pillars showcasing remarkable craftsmanship.
  4. Prithivi Lingam: The presiding deity, Lord Ekambareswarar, is represented by a lingam made of earth (Prithivi Lingam). It is believed to have been installed by Goddess Parvati herself.
  5. Akanda Deepam: The temple is famous for the Akanda Deepam, a ritual in which a massive lamp is lit with ghee on the top of the temple's main gopuram. It remains perpetually lit and symbolizes the eternal presence of Lord Shiva.
  6. Silk Weaving Tradition: Kanchipuram is renowned for its silk sarees, and the Ekambareswarar Temple plays a significant role in this tradition. The temple priests are known for weaving the silk threads used in the sarees, which are offered to the deities and then sold to devotees.

Palani Murugan Temple, Palani:

  1. The Palani Murugan Temple, situated in Palani, is dedicated to Lord Murugan, the son of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Here are five notable aspects of this temple:
  2. Hilltop Temple: The temple is located atop a hill called Palani Malai, and devotees have to climb 659 steps to reach the sanctum sanctorum. The pilgrimage to the temple is considered spiritually rewarding.
  3. Golden Chariot Procession: The temple is famous for its annual Thai Pusam festival, during which a grand procession takes place. The deity is carried in a golden chariot, and devotees participate in elaborate rituals and offerings.
  4. Panchamirtam: The temple is known for its Panchamirtam, a sacred mixture of five ingredients (jaggery, ghee, honey, banana, and cardamom) that is believed to have healing properties. It is distributed as prasadam to devotees.
  5. Navapashanam: The temple possesses nine sacred stones, known as Navapashanam, which are believed to have medicinal and curative properties. Devotees often seek blessings by touching these stones.
  6. Skanda Sashti Festival: Skanda Sashti, a six-day festival dedicated to Lord Murugan, is celebrated with great fervor in the Palani Murugan Temple. It commemorates Lord Murugan's victory over the demon Surapadman.
